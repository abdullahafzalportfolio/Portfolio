Common questions

Answers before you ask.

Straight answers on how I work, what I deliver and what to expect — across Shopify, SEO, marketing, speed, conversion, AI and growth.

Do you build new Shopify stores or improve existing ones?

Both. I build new stores from scratch, replatform from other systems, and optimize existing Shopify stores that are underperforming on speed, SEO or conversion.

How do you make a Shopify store faster?

By trimming app bloat, using a lightweight theme, optimizing images to modern formats, deferring non-critical scripts, and tuning Core Web Vitals — measured before and after so the gain is real.

Will a new Shopify build hurt my existing SEO?

Not if it’s handled properly. I preserve URLs where possible, map redirects for anything that changes, keep your metadata and structured data intact, and monitor Search Console through the transition.

What is Shopify SEO and does it differ from regular SEO?

The fundamentals are the same, but Shopify has platform-specific quirks — collection and product URL structure, duplicate-content traps, and theme-level markup. Shopify SEO means getting those details right on top of solid on-page work.

How does the Google map pack actually rank businesses?

Broadly, on relevance, distance and prominence — a complete Google Business Profile, consistent citations, genuine reviews, and locally relevant on-page content all feed those signals.

Can you help me rank in a city where I don’t have an office?

If you legitimately serve that area, yes — through service-area strategy, relevant local landing pages and proper profile settings. I don’t use fake addresses or any tactic that risks a suspension.

Do you write fake reviews or buy citations?

Never. Fake reviews violate Google’s policies and put your profile at risk. I build ethical review-request workflows that earn genuine reviews from real customers.

What does technical SEO actually cover?

Everything that helps search engines crawl, render and understand your site: site speed and Core Web Vitals, crawlability and indexation, structured data, internal linking, canonicalization and clean site architecture.

My traffic dropped after a migration. Can you help?

Usually, yes. Post-migration drops often trace to broken redirects, lost metadata, indexation gaps or damaged structured data. I audit the migration, fix the root causes and monitor recovery.

What are Core Web Vitals and why do they matter?

They’re Google’s measures of loading, interactivity and visual stability (LCP, INP and CLS). They influence both rankings and conversions — a slow, unstable page loses visitors and revenue.

How fast should my website load?

Aim for a largest-contentful-paint under about 2.5 seconds on mobile, and ideally faster. The exact number matters less than the trend — every improvement tends to help conversion.

What is conversion rate optimization (CRO)?

It’s the practice of turning more of your existing traffic into customers — through better UX, clearer messaging, trust signals and tested changes. Often the fastest route to more revenue without more spend.

How do you run a conversion test properly?

With a clear hypothesis, enough traffic to reach significance, one meaningful change at a time, and a defined success metric — then I roll out winners and document what we learned.

What does digital marketing include in your engagements?

Strategy and channel selection, an SEO and content engine, conversion-focused landing pages, email and lifecycle basics, and the analytics to measure it all — chosen by what fits your business, not a fixed template.

Do you use AI to do the work?

I use AI as leverage for repetitive, high-volume tasks — always with human review. Strategy, judgment and quality control stay human. AI is a tool, not a replacement for expertise.

What kind of tasks can you automate?

Reporting, lead capture and routing, follow-up sequences, content production pipelines and repetitive operations work — anything high-volume and rule-based, with quality guardrails built in.

How long until I see SEO results?

Speed and conversion fixes can show quickly; SEO compounds over months. I give realistic timelines up front and never promise overnight rankings — anyone who does isn’t being honest.

Do you guarantee rankings or revenue?

No honest specialist guarantees exact rankings — Google doesn’t allow it. What I guarantee is sound method, transparency and effort measured against clear KPIs.

How do you measure success?

Against real KPIs in Search Console, GA4 and, where possible, your revenue. If a result can’t be seen in the data, it doesn’t count.

Am I working with you directly or a team?

Directly with me. The person who scopes your project does the work — no account managers, no juniors relaying messages.

Do you work with businesses outside your country?

Yes — I work with clients across the USA, Canada, the UK and Australia, and I keep communication timezone-aware so nothing stalls.

How much do your services cost?

It depends on scope. I work on fixed-scope projects and monthly retainers, and you’ll get a clear quote after our call — no hidden surprises.

Can you work alongside my existing developer or marketer?

Yes. I regularly collaborate with in-house teams and other vendors when it serves the outcome, and I’m clear about who owns what.

What’s the fastest way to grow my business online?

Usually by fixing conversion and speed on the traffic you already have before spending more to acquire it. We start where the biggest, quickest lever is — then compound it.
